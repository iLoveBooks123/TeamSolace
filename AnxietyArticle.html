Handling Anxiety Disorders

By The Solace Team | Read Time: 1 min

← Back to Blog

Anxiety disorder is when you respond to situations with fear, you overreact and can’t control your responses to situations. Some symptoms are feeling helplessness or nervous, sweating, trembling, experiencing panic or dange or hyperventilating. Understanding these basic elements is the first step toward effective management.

You should use relaxation techniques such as deep breathing and meditation or engage in physical activity. These immediate steps help manage acute symptoms. Consider peer support by asking a friend or family member for help to build a support system.

How to cope with them is eating healthy foods, learn about your disorder, make sleep a priority, have a treatment plan, and write in a journal. These lifestyle changes and structured approaches form the foundation of long-term management and well-being.
