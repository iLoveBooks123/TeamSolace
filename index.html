Our Mission

Solace was created by young people, for young people. We understand the unique pressures of school, social life, and growing up today. Our mission is to provide an accessible, trustworthy, and non-judgemental space where you can find genuine support.

We believe that mental health is just as important as physical health. That's why we offer tools, professional check-ins, and a supportive community. Everything here is designed to help you build resilience, manage stress, and thrive.

We are here for you, always.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is mental health? +

Mental health refers to a person’s emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, act, handle stress, relate to others, and make decisions.

Who can I contact if I need help right now?? +

If you’re feeling overwhelmed or unsafe, call or text 988 to reach the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. If it’s an emergency, call 911 right away.

What are common mental health disorders? +

Common mental health disorders include depression, anxiety disorders, bipolar disorder, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), eating disorders, and schizophrenia. These conditions can affect mood, behavior, and daily functioning.

What are signs that someone may be struggling with mental health? +

Warning signs can include persistent sadness, extreme mood changes, withdrawal from friends, changes in sleep or appetite, difficulty concentrating, or feelings of hopelessness.

Can mental health disorders be treated? +

Yes. Mental health disorders can be treated through therapy, medication, lifestyle changes, and support systems. Early treatment often leads to better outcomes.

How does stress affect mental health? +

Long-term stress can increase the risk of anxiety, depression, and burnout. Managing stress through healthy coping strategies is important for maintaining good mental health.
